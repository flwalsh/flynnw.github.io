The Simple d12 System

Philosophy

Simple d12 is a minimalist, setting-agnostic, abstractly realistic system. It is designed for ease of character generation and minimal player downtime. Combat is authentically fast-paced, rushed, and lethal.

Character Generation

  1. Determine a setting-appropriate background, consisting of a character’s:

    1. upbringing, including:
      • nationality
      • social class
    2. education (formal or informal, if any)
    3. previous endeavors (if any)
      • e.g. military service

  2. Select a vocation, specialized to the period. Vocations include but are by no means limited to:

    Actor, Bureaucrat, Crafter, Criminal, Doctor, Engineer, Entrepreneur, Farmer, Investigator, Merchant, Police, Politician, Priest, Scientist, Soldier, Spy

    Specialized, these could be alchemist or xenobiologist (scientist), trauma surgeon or plague doctor (doctor), landsknecht or bomb disposal specialist (solider), machinist or fletcher (craftsman).

  3. Allocate 18 points between analytical (An), emotional (Em), and physical (Ph) aptitudes. Values are roughly interpretable as below (0 in any means death).

Value Analytical Emotional Physical
1 comatose aphasic infirm
2 imbecilic robotic feeble
3 stupid irksome sickly
4 slow awkward weak
5-6 normal normal normal
7 clever likable tough
8 smart popular athletic
9 brilliant enchanting powerful
10 genius empathic herculean
  1. Determine your character’s values, goals, appearance, etc. and describe what is apparent to others.

Taking Actions

For any action where success cannot be assumed, roll 1d12.

  • +1d12 if trained in or familiar with the task, as would be appropriate for the character’s background, vocation, or from prior game experience.
  • For social interactions, this could mean being part of the same class or subculture as a person you’re trying to convince or deceive.
  • +1d12 if prepared, e.g. an aimed shot, a strike after a feint, or working without any time pressure.
  • Training and preparation are subject to GM discretion.

The target of a roll is the character’s most relevant aptitude (An, Em, or Ph).

A die passes if its value is equal to or below the target and fails if greater. For actions that aren’t attacks, the outcome of a roll is as follows:

roll result
multiple dice fail something goes wrong
once die fails failure
once die passes success with a caveat
two dice pass success
three dice pass critical success

Combat

Combat proceeds in rounds corresponding to ~3 seconds in game time.

  1. At the beginning of each round, the GM outlines the situation and may draw or update a map.
    • “The car you’re following veers off the bridge as gunfire erupts from the adjacent apartment block.”
  2. The GM begins a stopwatch as each player has 3An seconds to ask further questions, in the order of lowest to highest Em:
    • “Which window is the gunfire coming from?”
  3. Next, in the same order, players have 3An seconds to describe a move consisting of two actions (~3 seconds in game), which are simultaneously resolved alongside any NPCs’ by the GM.

Actions may include:

  • Attacking, e.g., shooting, punching, and stabbing, generally a Ph roll.
  • Preparation, primarily aiming or feinting, which adds a preparation d12 to the next attack on a specific target. Preparations do not stack.
    • Aiming is necessary to avoid the chance of friendly fire into a melee, otherwise an An roll is required.
  • Evasion, including diving for cover, dodging backward, falling prone, blocking, or parrying. Mechanically, a Ph roll where each passing die negates a successful attack die that would otherwise hit.
    • It’s not possible to evade twice in one round, but one can spend an action to prepare an evasion.
  • Movement, 4m, 8m if moved the previous round.
    • Two actions moving is equivalent to 12mph
    • On a battle mat, 1 square/hex = 2m is recommended
  • Inquiry Asking additional follow up questions (3An s).
    • “Do the guards’ uniforms look real?”
  • Another action simple enough you can describe and perform it in the alloted time.
    • includes reloading

E.g., a player may duck behind a wall and fire (evasion + attacking) or simply stand up and unload (attacking twice). A swordsman can parry (evasion) while backing up tower stairs (movement) or feint (preparation) and lunge at the enemy (attack).

Without armor or other modifiers, each successful attack die deals one point of damage to a target’s Ph. Ph can be regenerated using medical technology or resting for a realistically long time.

Combat Modifiers

Various weapons may add to attack roll aptitudes or increase the number of dice rolled.

Example Gear

Equipment may reduce an attacker’s target or negate dice that would otherwise hit.

Item -Target -d12 Notes
Round shield 1 0 if facing attacker, max 8d
Full shield 2 0 if facing attacker, -2m move, max 12d
Basic armor 0 1
Full armor 0 2 prohibits swimming, -1m move

Basic armor could be a chain shirt or a bullet proof vest. Full armor would be plate mail or riot gear.

Cover

Attacking a target behind cover reduces the attacker’s effective aptitude by -2 for improvised cover (walls, windows, hostages) and -3 for fortifications (castle crenelations, pillboxes, etc.) Shooting through cover may be possible if enough dice hit at the GM’s discretion.

Attacking a single-moving target lowers effective aptitude by 1. A double-moving target is -2 to hit.

Combat Target:

In sum, you roll

1 + proficiency + preparation + weapon damage

d12. Dice that are under

Ph + weapon mod + distance mod - cover/shield - movement

succeed. Armor may negate successful dice.

Equipment

By default, money is in terms of a setting appropriate currency (e.g. silver, pence, sesterii, credits, quants) approximately equivalent to USD/EUR with prices derived from real world data.

Magic

If magic (including deities) exist in the setting, it should be truly magical, i.e. mystical, poorly understood, and most importantly dangerous. It should not be mechanical or predictable in any way.
